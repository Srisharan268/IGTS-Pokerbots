2.A:
exactly any K people contribute and exactly no one
 contributes are the only two nash equilibria.

proof:
if >K contribute or if non-zero people <K contribute,
any person contributing has incentive to change his 
decision assuming everyone else does not change. 
Hence >K or {1,2,..,k-1} cannot be psne.

0 and K are PSNE because no one has incentive to 
change their decision.

same analysis as in 2.A
