player going second will win

strategy:
player 2 copies whichever move player 1 made

proof:
if it there are even number or rocks left in each 
of the piles at the start of a turn, then there is 
no possible way there will be even number of rocks 
(i.e 0,0) in both piles after the turn.

as we already start with even number of rocks in 
both piles, as long as player 2 copies player 1's 
move, there will always be an even number of rocks 
in both piles at the beggining of player 1s turn.

Hence player 1 cannot win.
